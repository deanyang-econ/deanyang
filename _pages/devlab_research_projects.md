* **[The Long Shadow of Early Education: Evidence from a Natural Experiment in the Philippines](/deanyang/files/workingpapers/lloyd-yang-2025-long-shadow.pdf)**. A policy-induced decline in early education quality in Philippine schools caused students to complete approximately 0.3 fewer years of schooling, showing lasting negative effects on long-run educational attainment. *DevLab members: Thomas Lloyd (co-author); Yu Shi (RA).*

* **[The Price of Faith: Economic Costs and Religious Group Membership in Sub-Saharan Africa](/deanyang/files/workingpapers/montero-yang-yentzen-2026-price-of-faith.pdf)**. When the Seventh-Day Adventist church's religious prohibitions become economically costly in suitable growing regions, membership growth declines and the church adapts by emphasizing educational and health services over doctrinal observance. *DevLab members: Triana Yentzen (co-author); Robert Beckemeyer (RA), Nicholas Martens (RA).*

* **[Ancient Epics in the Television Age: Religious Identity and the Rise of Hindu Nationalism in India](/deanyang/files/workingpapers/yang-saia-kovvuri-ahmed-brimble-2026-ancient-epics.pdf)**. Exposure to the televised Ramayan in 1987 led to persistently strengthened Hindu religious identity, increased communal violence in the short term, and greater long-run electoral support for the BJP. *DevLab members: Paul Brimble (co-author); Arushi Sharma (RA).*

* **[War Mobilization and Economic Development: World War II and Structural Transformation in India](/deanyang/files/workingpapers/parvathaneni-yang-2024-war-mobilization-india.pdf)**. Districts with higher WWII procurement spending saw lasting structural transformation away from agriculture toward industry and services, with higher consumption, urbanization, and in-migration more than 60 years later. *DevLab members: Aneesha Parvathaneni (co-author); Rohan Bhargava (RA), Zara Jacob (RA), Jinghao Liu (RA), Brady Nichols (RA), Ann Yang (RA).*

* **[Global Jobs Africa](https://www.socialscienceregistry.org/trials/16818)**. This project tests whether targeted training, mentoring, and data-driven guidance can reduce barriers facing workers in Sub-Saharan Africa — particularly women — who seek to participate in the global remote job market. *DevLab members: Heesung Kim (co-author), Nathan Sunday (co-author); Matthew Burger (RA), Hadin Sayed (RA), Yvette Zhang (RA).*

* **[Returns to Information in Hiring](https://www.socialscienceregistry.org/trials/11630)**. This project evaluates different approaches to screening job applicants for health promoter positions in Mozambique, measuring how hiring methods affect worker effectiveness in delivering HIV/AIDS-related public health services. *DevLab members: Yara Gomes (pre-doctoral scholar), Ryan McWay (pre-doctoral scholar), Rita Neves (pre-doctoral scholar); Nyah Phillips (RA), Olivia Woodring (RA).*

* **The Effects of the Green Revolution on Long-Run Development: Evidence from India**. In India, improvements in agricultural productivity due to the Green Revolution increase deforestation, but industrialization helps preserve forests. *DevLab members: Joaquin Endara (co-author), Sameer Nair-Desai (co-author); Evan Evans (RA), Raelynn Li (RA), Laura Wang (RA).*
